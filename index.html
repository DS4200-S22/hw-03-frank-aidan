HTML+CSS & Marks+Channels

  • The data in this visualization consist of ordinal attributes on the x-axis and continuous attributes on the y-axis, both acting as items. In the stacked bar chart, the marks consist of the bars themselves and the areas they inhabit. The channels consist of the colors chosen, the rectangular shape of the bars, their length/size, and their position relative to one another.

  • The data in this visualization consist of two sets of ordinal attributes on the y-axis (A-E and 1-5) as well as time on the x-axis as an ordinal attribute. It also consists of links relating the ranking of nodes A-E over time. In the bump chart, the marks consist of the points in space, the area they inhabit, and the lines connecting them. The channels consist of ordered position based on year, the colors differentiating A-E, and the circular shape of the points.

  • The data in this visualization consist of nodes and links relating those nodes together in a hierarchical manner. In the mind map, the marks consist of the space inhabited by the ovals, as well as the lines connecting them. The channels consist of the colors used to differentiate the theme, category, and ideas, as well as the ovals and their position in space relative to other ovals.

  • The data in this visualization consist of four continuous attributes acting as items. In the compound bubble and pie chart, the marks consist of the space inhabited by the circles and the points present in space. The channels consist of the colors showing different versions of an attribute, the angle of the slices of the pie, the size of each pie, the circular shape, and the position of the pie in space.
